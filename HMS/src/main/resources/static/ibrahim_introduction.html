Introduction

I am a Jordanian with Syrian roots, born and raised in the capital of Jordan. My familial connection to Syria runs deep – my father, having spent 18 years in Syria, and my mother, although born in Jordan, also traces her roots back to Syria. The name "Bondokji" is a tribute to my great-grandparents, renowned for their profession in selling rifles during the Ottoman Empire. Pronounced as "Bondokia," this legacy has given rise to our family name, "Bondokji."

From my earliest memories as a child, my fascination with computers and technology was evident. I was naturally drawn to dismantling gadgets and understanding how they worked. Building things, whether it was assembling a computer from spare parts or experimenting with different technologies, became a favorite pastime. This early passion for tinkering and exploration laid the foundation for my journey as a software engineer. As I grew older, my enthusiasm evolved into a dedicated pursuit of knowledge in the ever-evolving world of technology.

As a software engineer, I embody reliability, diligence, and a strong sense of responsibility. My unwavering drive propels me to craft software solutions that address real-world challenges, employing high-quality code and adhering to software design principles. I bring confidence and motivation to the professional realm, backed by a robust understanding of computing fundamentals, proficiency in object-oriented programming using Python, expertise in machine learning and data processing, and a solid grasp of UNIX-based systems. My toolkit extends to the utilization of software automation tools like Jenkins, Docker, and version control. As a dynamic team player, I excel in leading teams to plan, execute, and achieve high-quality project objectives.

Technology, for me, is a powerful equalizer and a catalyst for progress. Growing up in Jordan, a developing country facing societal and environmental challenges, I witnessed its transformation into a prominent technology hub. This progress, attracting global tech giants like Amazon, Microsoft, and Oracle, owes its success to access to information technology. The internet age empowered young talents nationwide to learn beyond traditional curricula, fostering my passion and instilling a sense of responsibility to contribute to constructive change.

My educational journey commenced with an undergraduate degree in Computer Science from the esteemed German Jordanian University, a top engineering and IT institution in Jordan. During my senior year, an internship at Mercedes-Benz Group’s Connected Cars division exposed me to software engineering. There, I successfully implemented a user analytics tool, showcasing my ability to collaborate within diverse teams.

Driven by a thirst for knowledge, I integrated machine learning into my capstone project. This knowledge became instrumental when I assumed a leadership role at NAFITH, a logistics company. Despite resource constraints, I led a team to develop a cutting-edge license plate recognition system using computer vision and machine learning, transforming it into a commercially successful product.

My professional journey continued at MBC Group, the largest media company in the MENA region. In my role as an AWS and Media Engineer, I manage server-less workflows and micro-services for video processing using AWS. Despite being new to AWS, my proactive approach has positioned me as a leader within the team, responsible for enhancing workflows to align with software engineering best practices.

Motivated by a continuous pursuit of self-development, currently I am enrolled in the Master’s program in Management Information Systems at the University of Illinois at Chicago (UIC). This program aligns with my aspirations to specialize in both managerial and technical aspects of Information Technology.
